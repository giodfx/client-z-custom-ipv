Understanding Rape Culture

Sexual Violence in Our Society

Violence against women is based upon the idea of unequal power between men and women, a message that has been used by society throughout history.

Rape culture is a term used to describe an environment where sexual violence against women is normalized and excused in the media and popular culture. It is perpetuated through:

1
Misogynistic Language
Words that demean and devalue women
2
Objectification of Women's Bodies
Treating women as objects rather than people
3
Glamorization of Sexual Violence
Portraying violence as acceptable or desirable

The Rape Culture Pyramid

The Rape Culture Pyramid, created by the 11th Principle: Consent advocacy group, shows how gender stereotypes can perpetuate abusive and violent behaviors at many levels.

Click each tier to explore how behaviors escalate

Key Insight: All layers are interconnected. To prevent violence at the top, we must challenge harmful beliefs and behaviors at the foundation.

Once you know what rape culture is, it may be overwhelming to see how common these behaviors are and how "normal" they are considered in our society.

Challenging Rape Culture

Taking Action Against Harmful Norms

It is not easy to challenge rape culture, especially for those who have intentionally or unintentionally perpetuated it. It is important, however, to still have these conversations and call out sexist language or behaviors, no matter how small they seem.

Impact on All Genders

It is also important to recognize these harmful beliefs affect individuals of any gender, not just women and girls. Because of this, there are many organizations led by men that focus on spreading awareness about the impact of rigid gender stereotypes and the effect it can have on men and boys.

Gender Stereotypes Harm Everyone:

  • Boys and men are told to suppress emotions ("boys don't cry")
  • Men are expected to be aggressive and dominant
  • Showing vulnerability is seen as weakness
  • These expectations contribute to violence and harm

The "Boys Don't Cry" campaign from White Ribbon shows how gender stereotypes impact the way men and boys act in our society. It captures many of the concepts in the Rape Culture Pyramid and how harmful behaviors and mindsets can build to sexual violence.

Prevention Through Education

Preventing Sexual Violence and Rape Culture

The "Boys Don't Cry" initiative seeks to prevent sexual violence by breaking down common stereotypes and behaviors that often reinforce rape culture. By educating the public through common images and familiar language, this effort aims to lead to awareness and prevention behaviors rather than behaviors that reinforce rape culture.

Ultimately, prevention must include education, both formal and informal.

How Allies and Advocates Can Participate:

- Talking about rape culture and gender-based violence with others - Break the silence and normalize these important conversations

- Challenging sexist comments in conversations - Speak up when you hear jokes, language, or attitudes that perpetuate harm

- Knowing your local resources - Be prepared to connect survivors with appropriate support
