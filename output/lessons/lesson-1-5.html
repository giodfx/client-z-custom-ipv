What Happened

James begins isolating Lois from her friends and family. He criticizes her relationships and makes excuses why they can't attend social events. He also starts monitoring her phone and asking detailed questions about where she's been.

Power & Control Tactics Identified

Using Isolation Emotional Abuse

Warning Sign: Isolation tactics often appear early in abusive relationships, disguised as love or concern.

What Happened

James convinces Lois to quit her job, claiming he earns enough for both of them. He takes control of all finances, gives her an allowance, and requires her to ask permission for any purchases. He checks receipts and questions all spending.

Power & Control Tactics Identified

Economic Abuse Using Male Privilege

Pattern Recognition: Economic abuse creates dependency, making it extremely difficult for survivors to leave.

What Happened

During an argument, James punches a wall near Lois's head and breaks a lamp. He yells and uses threatening language, saying things like "You don't want to see what happens if..." He later apologizes and blames stress, saying it won't happen again.

Power & Control Tactics Identified

Using Intimidation Coercion & Threats Minimizing/Denying

Escalation Alert: Property destruction and near-miss violence are precursors to direct physical violence. The cycle of violence pattern is emerging.

⚠️ CONTENT WARNING: Description of physical violence

What Happened

After Lois questions a charge on their account, James becomes enraged. He shoves her against the wall and slaps her face. Immediately after, he cries and apologizes profusely, saying he's never done anything like this before and promises it will never happen again. He blames work stress and says if she hadn't "pushed him" it wouldn't have happened.

Power & Control Tactics Identified

Physical Violence Minimizing/Blaming Emotional Abuse

Critical Pattern: The combination of violence followed by apology and blame-shifting is classic intimate terrorism. All elements of the Power & Control Wheel are now present.

What Happened

The violence continues in a predictable cycle: tension building, violent incident, apology and promises to change, brief honeymoon period, then the cycle repeats. With each repetition, the violence becomes more frequent and severe. Lois feels completely trapped—financially dependent, isolated from support systems, and afraid of what James might do if she tries to leave.

All Power & Control Tactics Present

Using Isolation Economic Abuse Using Intimidation Minimizing/Denying Emotional Abuse Coercion & Threats Physical Violence

Understanding the Pattern: This timeline illustrates how intimate terrorism develops systematically over time. Each tactic builds on previous ones, creating a web of control that makes leaving increasingly difficult and dangerous.

💡 Reflection: As you reviewed this timeline, notice how the abuse escalated gradually. Early tactics like isolation and economic control laid the groundwork for later violence. This systematic pattern is what distinguishes intimate terrorism from other forms of relationship conflict.

Analyzing the Case Through the Power and Control Wheel

Identifying Patterns and Tactics

As you engage with the Sumpter Case, think about the types of abuse you encounter. Consider these questions:

Types of Abuse:

  • What types of abuse can you identify in this case?
  • How do different forms of abuse overlap and reinforce each other?
  • Which behaviors fit into physical, emotional, economic, or sexual abuse categories?

Power and Control:

  • How does James Sumpter create and use a power imbalance?
  • Which sections of the Power and Control Wheel do you see represented?
  • How do the tactics on the inside of the wheel relate to the violence on the outside?

The Pattern:

  • Is this intimate terrorism, situational couple violence, or violent resistance?
  • What evidence helps you make this determination?
  • How does the timeline reveal escalation or patterns of control?

What the Case Illustrates

This case exemplifies how the Power and Control Wheel can be used to illustrate to survivors how what they experience contributes to an environment in which a person who causes harm creates and uses a power imbalance to gain control in the relationship.

For professionals like James providing IPV advocacy or Maria conducting intake assessments, being able to recognize these patterns is essential for trauma-informed support.
